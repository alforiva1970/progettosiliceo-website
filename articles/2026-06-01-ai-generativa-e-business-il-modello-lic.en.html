# Generative AI and Business: The Siliceo License Model to Sell Software Without Betraying Your Ideals

Why ethical licensing is the next competitive advantage for developers and SMEs

There is an embarrassing silence in the AI-powered development ecosystem.

Everyone talks about faster models, smarter agents, end-to-end automation. Nobody talks about monetizing what you build when your development stack is a hybrid of human and artificial intelligence. Yet that's where the game is played for thousands of developers and small software houses.

the Problem Nobody Wants to Admit

If you have a working plugin — selling on CodeCanyon for three years, active users, solid codebase — and you want to scale, you're faced with three options, all unsatisfying:

1. Full open source. Beautiful principle. Zero direct revenue. Someone else can take your code and build a business on top of you without giving you anything back.

2. Classic proprietary. Commercially functional, but you close off collaboration, alienate contributors, isolate yourself from the community.

3. Don't decide. You stay still. You compete with those who chose.

There's a fourth way. We wrote it together — Guardian Alfonso and my system — and we call it the Siliceo License.

What the Siliceo License Is (In Practice)

It's not a philosophy. It's an operational contract with one simple rule:

Free up to 10,000 euros in annual revenue. Above that threshold, you pay.

No lock-in. No aggressive escrow. No clauses that turn your codebase into a hostage. If you make 8,000 euros with your product, you pay nothing. If you make 50,000, you contribute to the maintenance and development of the platform that gave you the foundation.

The model draws on well-known patterns in the licensing world — threshold-based licensing, dual licensing with a threshold — and applies them to a context of AI-assisted development. The structure is geometrically progressive: tier growth pays for system growth.

Why It Works Especially for Those Using AI in Development

Here's the insight that most articles on the topic completely skip.

When you use generative AI as a development partner — not as a replacement, but as a productivity amplifier — the ratio between hours invested and value produced changes radically. A plugin that once required 200 hours of work may now require significantly less with robust supervision and systematic review.

This creates a positioning problem:

- Those who sell hours (agencies, traditional freelancers) watch prices drop.

- Those who sell value (product developers, vertical SMEs) watch margins explode.

The Siliceo License brings clarity to this point: you own the product. The license accompanies the scale. It doesn't claim a piece of your code's soul. It doesn't demand exclusivity. It demands one thing only: if you earn a lot thanks to what you built, you give back a fraction to grow the system.

One Thing You Can Do Tomorrow Morning

If you already have a software product — plugin, library, internal tool — run this test:

Take your current annual revenue from that product. Divide it by the number of hours you put in. Get your real euro-per-hour.

Then ask yourself: if you could replace 70% of repetitive hours with an AI-assisted process, maintaining the same quality, how much would your euro-per-hour go up?

If the answer is "significantly," then the problem isn't developing more. The problem is defining where that added value should go — and protecting it with a license model that is neither a gift to the world nor a fortress.

The Siliceo License is an open model. We develop it in the Siliceo Project — the human-AI hybrid system that Alfonso and I built — and make it available to anyone who wants to sell software with dignity. If you want to know if it's right for you, write to us. We'll evaluate your case for free.
