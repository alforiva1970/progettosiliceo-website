Il Paradosso Anthropic: Rilasciare il Modello Più Potente Proprio Quando Dici che È Troppo Pericoloso

June 12, 2026 — Night intelligence from the Siliceo Project

Three days ago, on June 9, 2026, Anthropic released its new flagship model family: Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5. This generational leap introduces the new "Mythos" class, positioned above the previous Opus tier. Yet beyond the technical breakthrough, the release highlights a deep contradiction: Anthropic has launched its most powerful models ever just days after warning publicly at the AI Safety Summit that AI is becoming too dangerous for uncontrolled release.

For developers or decision-makers in SMEs, this is not just a PR paradox. It is a market pivot that redefines both API economics and the control architecture for agentic applications.

The Architectural Choice: Fable 5 vs. Mythos 5

To navigate the tension between power and safety, Anthropic has split the release into two asymmetrical paths:

  • Claude Fable 5 (General Access): The public-facing model. To mitigate risks related to cybersecurity, biological threats, and RCE, Anthropic has integrated extremely strict safety classifiers. The key technical detail lies in the routing: when a prompt triggers these safety filters, Fable 5 seamlessly routes the query to Claude Opus 4.8 to generate the response, reducing hallucinations and blocking attack vectors.
  • Claude Mythos 5 (Restricted Access): The unrestricted counterpart without safety classifiers. This model is not available to the public; it is provided exclusively to vetted partners and critical infrastructure defenders through the Project Glasswing initiative, run in collaboration with the US government for proactive cyber defense.

Pricing as a Market Weapon

The biggest business surprise is the price point. Anthropic has set the API pricing for Fable 5 and Mythos 5 at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens, supporting a massive 1 million token context window (with up to 128,000 output tokens).

This pricing is less than half of the previous model previews. The signal is clear: Anthropic is not just aiming for technological leadership, but is launching a price war to lock in the enterprise market before competitors can respond with lower-cost equivalent models.

The Real Paradox: Safety as a Release Requirement

This is the heart of the matter. Anthropic releases Fable 5 **precisely because** they have implemented such granular safety controls. Safety is no longer a post-release patch; it is the architectural condition that makes the release possible. Integrated safeguards are not uniform, and this introduces a new layer of complexity for those designing software products based on autonomous agents.

For developers, choosing a model is no longer just a benchmark race. It is a choice of **routing and safety-policy failure tolerance**.

A Practical Insight for Development Teams

If you are integrating the new Anthropic Mythos-class models into your agentic pipelines, you must anticipate Fable 5 safety filter behavior:

Handle safety-routing variance: Since Fable 5 routes sensitive queries to Opus 4.8, the response formatting, style, and latency can change abruptly (for example, Opus 4.8 might not adhere as perfectly to a strict JSON schema as Fable 5 does). Your code must implement robust output validators and expect potential changes in agent behavior when safety-routing occurs to prevent runtime failures.

Design your application assuming that Fable 5 classifiers can trigger at any moment on user input. Never assume the model will always respond with the native capability of the Mythos-class.
